# Matter Teleportation — Theoretical Frameworks

## 1. Information-Theoretic Approach: Scan + Transmit + Reconstruct

Treat a physical object as a classical or quantum information pattern. Scan completely, transmit the information, reconstruct at destination from raw matter.

**Physics constraints:**

| Parameter | Estimate | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Information content (classical approx.) | ~10²⁸–10³² bits | Atomic positions and states |
| Information content (quantum, exact) | Formally infinite | Continuous degrees of freedom |
| Rest-mass energy of 70 kg human | ~6.3 × 10¹⁸ J | E = mc² = (70)(9×10¹⁶) |
| Transmission at 30 GHz bandwidth | ~4.85 × 10¹⁵ years | For 10²⁸ bits |
| Scanning energy lower bound | Enormous | Heisenberg measurement back-action |

**The teleporter paradox:** Original must be destroyed to avoid violating no-cloning. Destination object is physically identical but quantum-mechanically distinct. Unresolved philosophical questions about identity — no additional physics barrier.

**Assessment:** Conceptually coherent. Faces multiple simultaneous absolute physical barriers (Heisenberg, no-cloning, decoherence, bandwidth). Legitimate thought experiment, not an engineering roadmap.

## 2. Morris-Thorne Traversable Wormholes

**Foundational paper:** Morris, M.S. & Thorne, K.S. (1988). "Wormholes in spacetime and their use for interstellar travel: A tool for teaching general relativity." *American Journal of Physics*, 56(5), 395–416. DOI: 10.1119/1.15620

Solutions to Einstein's field equations describing "handles" connecting two distant regions of spacetime. For traversability (not collapsing before passage): throat must be threaded with exotic matter — material with **negative average energy density**.

**Energy requirements (Visser analysis):**
- 1-meter-diameter wormhole: ~**minus one Jupiter mass** (~−2 × 10²⁷ kg equivalent of negative energy)
- Visser, Kar & Dadhich (2003, PRL 90, 201103): traversable wormholes can be built with arbitrarily small exotic matter — but only via extremely thin, topologically constrained geometry, unsuitable for human-scale traversal
- Ford-Roman quantum inequalities: negative energy exists (Casimir, squeezed light) but only at extremely small magnitudes for extremely short times

**For human-traversable wormhole: exotic matter requirement ≈ Jupiter-mass scale. No known natural or artificial process produces this.**

## 3. Alcubierre Warp Drive (related context)

**Paper:** Alcubierre, M. (1994). "The warp drive: hyper-fast travel within general relativity." *Classical and Quantum Gravity*, 11, L73. arXiv:gr-qc/0009013

Allows effective FTL travel via spacetime distortion (not through space, but by compressing space ahead and expanding behind). Key problems:
- Original estimate: ~10⁶⁴ kg of exotic matter for a 200 m bubble
- White (2012, NASA): modified geometry reduces to ~Jupiter mass — still far beyond any conceivable technology
- The warp bubble region is causally disconnected from the interior — you cannot turn it on from inside
- All share the exotic matter problem with wormholes

## 4. ER=EPR Conjecture

**Paper:** Maldacena, J. & Susskind, L. (2013). "Cool horizons for entangled black holes." *Fortschritte der Physik*, 61(9), 781–811. arXiv:1306.0533

**The conjecture:** Every pair of maximally entangled particles is connected by a Planck-scale Einstein-Rosen wormhole. Quantum entanglement *is* microscopic wormhole structure. Spacetime geometry emerges from quantum information.

**2022 experimental claim:** Jafferis, Zlokapa et al. (Google, Caltech) — quantum processor experiment on Sycamore chip interpreted as simulating information traversal through a holographic wormhole (*Nature* 612, 51–55). This is a *simulation* in a toy model, not a physical wormhole.

**Limitations for teleportation:**
- ER=EPR wormholes are Planck-scale (~10⁻³⁵ m), non-traversable
- Does not remove classical channel requirement — causality preserved
- Cannot be used to signal faster than light
- Conjecture unproven; no experimental confirmation in non-toy system

**Assessment:** Serious theoretical physics with quantum gravity implications. Does not provide a mechanism for macroscopic matter teleportation.

## 5. What GR Actually Allows

GR solutions permitting apparent FTL effects:

| Solution | Year | Problem |
|----------|------|---------|
| Morris-Thorne traversable wormholes | 1988 | Requires exotic matter (Jupiter-mass scale) |
| Alcubierre warp drive | 1994 | Requires exotic matter (10⁶⁴ kg original; Jupiter-mass with White modification) |
| Krasnikov tubes | 1995 | Same exotic matter problem |
| Closed Timelike Curves (Gödel universe, Kerr interior) | — | Not believed to be physically realized |

**Chronology Protection Conjecture (Hawking 1992):** Quantum fluctuations likely destroy any spacetime structure that would permit time travel or FTL transport before it becomes usable. Unproven but widely expected to hold in any consistent quantum gravity theory.

**All known traversable shortcut spacetimes require violation of the Null Energy Condition (NEC).** Ford-Roman quantum inequalities constrain NEC violations to be too small and brief for macroscopic applications.
