# Government & Intelligence Community Documents on Teleportation

## CIA STARGATE Program
**Years Active:** 1972–1995 | **Agencies:** CIA / DIA / U.S. Army | **Cost:** ~$20M  
**FOIA Status:** Bulk declassified January 2017 — [CIA FOIA Reading Room](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/collection/stargate)

The program's core claim: trained subjects could perceive distant locations/events with no known sensory channel ("remote viewing"). No mainstream physics claims were made formally. Researchers Targ and Puthoff invoked quantum nonlocality and zero-point field theory as possible mechanisms — not formal program conclusions.

**Notable adjacent document:** "Analysis and Assessment of the Gateway Process" (CIA-RDP96-00788R001700210016-5, 1983, Lt. Col. Wayne McDonnell) — invokes holographic brain theory, quantum coherence, claims consciousness at resonant frequency could "escape restrictions of time and space." No experimental physics data. Speculative intelligence-officer synthesis of neuroscience and quantum mysticism.

**Final assessment (AIR 1995):** Remote viewing not demonstrated; no actionable intelligence produced. Program terminated. AIR report: CIA-RDP96-00791R000200180005-5.

**Assessment: Pseudoscience. Quantum nonlocality ≠ superluminal signaling.**

## DIA DIRD #18 — "Traversable Wormholes, Stargates, and Negative Energy"
**Author:** Eric W. Davis, Ph.D. (EarthTech International) | **Year:** 2010 | **Ref:** DIA-08-1004-004  
**Program:** AAWSAP (Advanced Aerospace Weapon System Applications Program)  
**PDF:** [DIA FOIA](https://www.dia.mil/FOIA/FOIA-Electronic-Reading-Room/FileId/170048/) | [Black Vault](https://documents2.theblackvault.com/documents/dia/AAWSAP-DIRDs/DIRD_18-DIRD_Traversible_Wormholes_Stargates_and_Negative_Energy.pdf)

**Physical claims:**
1. Traversable wormholes are permissible in GR (cites Morris-Thorne 1988) — **ESTABLISHED MATH**
2. Negative energy exists and is measurable (Casimir effect, squeezed light) — **ESTABLISHED PHYSICS**
3. Quantum inequalities (Ford-Roman) limit usable negative energy — **ESTABLISHED PHYSICS**
4. Casimir effect could be engineered to macro scale for wormhole stabilization — **HIGHLY SPECULATIVE**
5. Traversable wormholes could be engineered as transportation portals — **SPECULATIVE**

The physics framework is legitimate; the engineering leap is speculative.

## AFRL "Teleportation Physics Study" (2004)
**Author:** Eric W. Davis | **Report:** AFRL-PR-ED-TR-2003-0034 | **Date:** August 2004  
**Agency:** Air Force Research Laboratory, Edwards AFB — Space and Missile Propulsion Division  
**Cost:** $25,000 | **Length:** 88 pages  
**PDF:** [Black Vault](https://www.theblackvault.com/documentarchive/teleportation-physics-study-air-force-research-laboratory-august-2004-dr-eric-w-davis/) | [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/stream/Teleportation_Study_U.S.A.F.__unclassified/teleport_djvu.txt)

**Four teleportation paradigms addressed:**

| Type | Description | Assessment |
|------|-------------|------------|
| q-Teleportation | Quantum teleportation (Bennett 1993 protocol) | Mainstream physics, accurately reported |
| vm-Teleportation | Spacetime metric engineering / wormholes + Puthoff's PV-GR model | Wormhole GR = legitimate; PV-GR = non-mainstream |
| e-Teleportation | Extra dimensions / brane-world routing | Theoretical BSM physics, no experimental basis |
| p-Teleportation | Psychic / psychokinetic teleportation | **Pseudoscience** — Davis requested $7.5M to study this |

The p-Teleportation section cites uncontrolled Chinese government studies of "gifted children" teleporting objects and Soviet PK research. None independently replicated. AFRL explicitly distanced itself from this section. Lawrence Krauss and FAS publicly criticized the report.
